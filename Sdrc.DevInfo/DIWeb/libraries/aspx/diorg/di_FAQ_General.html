What is DevInfo?

 

DevInfo is a programme of support to UN member states that focuses on promoting the use of national statistics in evidence-based policy and planning dialogues at global, regional and national level.  This programme is termed the “DevInfo initiative”.  At its core, DevInfo programming is based around a database system that provides a method to organize, store and display data in a uniform format to facilitate data access at the country level among government institutions, their development partners and the general public.


DevInfo has been adapted from UNICEF ChildInfo database technology. DevInfo is an integrated desktop and web-enabled tool that assists countries in their reporting on the MDGs and other national and international targets. It has user-friendly features that can be used to produce tables, graphs, maps and other visual outputs for inclusion in reports, presentations and other advocacy or planning materials. The software supports standard indicators, such as those used for monitoring the MDGs at international level, yet can also be customized to fit the requirements of local indicator and monitoring frameworks at regional or country level.

How was DevInfo selected as a tool for MDG monitoring?


In the context of the UN inter-agency MDG working group, a small sub-group had been looking into the issue of a standard software package for monitoring the MDGs. In November 2002, the UNICEF Executive Director offered to the Secretary General to provide a software package to support the organization and display of data for monitoring goals for use by UN Country Teams and national governments. This package would be a modified and enhanced version of the ChildInfo package then being used by UNICEF. The new system would be called DevInfo. The MDG Working Group recommended that the offer be accepted, and the UNDG Programme Group subsequently endorsed this approach in 2003.

What is the vision for DevInfo?


DevInfo will contribute to setting universal standards for storage, access and dissemination of data on human development indicators, specifically the MDGs and other national and international targets. DevInfo aims to contribute to analytical processes by aiding the discovery and communication of meaningful patterns in data, in order to enhance the uptake of national statistics in evidence-based policy and decision-making.

Is DevInfo customizable to country specific situations?

Yes, DevInfo can be customized the meet the requirements of local institutions.  Indicator and goal frameworks, geographic area structures, images and colors can all be adjusted to meet user requirements.  The system can also handle multiple languages for Unicode-compliant fonts.

Can DevInfo be used to produce MDG Reports?


DevInfo allows the user to generate data reports, including those needed for MDG reporting. DevInfo provides simple facilities for querying the database and producing tables, graphs and maps for inclusion in MDG reports and exporting these to Word/Excel/PowerPoint.

Who should use DevInfo?

DevInfo does not attempt to supplant or replace existing efforts, such as well-established initiatives at the global, regional and country level, including thematic (sectoral) initiatives for monitoring individual goals or topics. DevInfo's specific purpose and added value is to provide a tool for compilation of existing data, identification of  data gaps if they exist and to provide a go-to source for data on national and international targets including the MDGs. It is also designed to make it easy for national entities to use the integrated database for advocacy with key decision-makers.

 

Typically, users would include:

  • UN country teams
  • National statistics offices
  • Government departments
  • Planning ministries
  • District planners
  • Media (to report on and track human development data)
  • Educational institutions (for data analysis and to help students have access to data)

Who owns DevInfo?

The DevInfo source code is the property of UNICEF, and is freely available under open source licensing.  To obtain a copy of the DevInfo source code and make contributions to its continuous improvement, please contact the DevInfo Global Administrator at UNICEF.

DevInfo can be used by anyone. UNICEF has no restrictions on the software, databases and their use. UNICEF’s mission is to advocate, mobilize resources and work with all UN agencies, governments and partners to ensure the realization of child rights. Information sharing/dissemination and encouraging partners to use data and statistics is one of the best ways to carry out this mission. At the country level, UNICEF and its partners have the right to freely distribute DevInfo.

What are the basic steps to successfully establish DevInfo in a country?

  • Assess data and monitoring needs
  • Identify UN focal points
  • Identify government focal points
  • Data inventory
  • Review and analysis of data quality from available sources
  • Selection of indicators in addition to the global MDG indicators
  • Data compilation
  • Database administration training
  • Data entry and verification
  • Database launch
  • Database user training

What modules make up the DevInfo software package?

DevInfo 7.0 comes in two separate applications:

  1. DevInfo User Application
  2. DevInfo Database Administration Application

The DevInfo 7.0 User application is deployed online and can either be hosted by the UN at no cost on devinfo.org or on a local server.  Previous versions including DevInfo 6.0/6.1 were primarily offline and required download and installation.


The DevInfo 7.0 Database Administration application has 11 modules  for data management:


  • Template - For identification of database structure, including use of local or standard indicators. A database template contains all of the following elements: indicators, indicator definitions, indicator sectors, indicator classes, area IDs, area names, units, subgroups.
  • Data Entry – For inclusion of data values, their sources and time periods.
  • Language - For translation of interface and database strings  into local languages.
  • Tools – For import, export and extraction of data from DevInfo datasets.
  • Reports – For producing basic reports on database statistics.
  • Metadata – For entering and editing database metadata.
  • Mapping – For management of area shape files and other customized map features.
  • Gallery – For preparation of searchable online galleries of presentation objects.
  • Data Exchange – For facilitating data exchange with other software formats.
  • EmergencyInfo – For accessing tools that facilitate data entry from mobile devices.
  • Standards – For exchange of DevInfo data in SDMX compliant format.

After deployment online, the administrator of a DevInfo 7 database can further customize the look and feel of the application through various settings.

How many geographic levels can be supported by DevInfo?


DevInfo supports an unlimited number of levels of geographical coverage (administrative area levels): from the global level to regional, sub-regional, national and sub-national down to sub-district and village levels.  Shape files from DevInfo databases at these levels are compiled in the DevInfo Digital Map Library (accessible via devinfo.org).

Can data be exported from DevInfo?

Yes, DevInfo data can be exported to XLS, HTML, PDF, CSV and XML files.

Can data be imported into DevInfo?


Yes, Data can be imported from spreadsheets in a standardized format.

Will support be provided for DevInfo?


Yes, the UN system, under the coordination of UNICEF, is committed to support DevInfo through under programming priorities of UN Country Teams.

How will training on DevInfo be provided?


The UN system will support the organization of global, regional and national-level training. This includes Training of Trainers and e-learning courses.  Training and support services are provided by reputable institutions and individuals that have been vetted by UNICEF.  For more information on institutions with Long Term Agreements with UNICEF, or individuals on the UNDG Experts Roster, please contact the DevInfo Global Administrator at UNICEF.
