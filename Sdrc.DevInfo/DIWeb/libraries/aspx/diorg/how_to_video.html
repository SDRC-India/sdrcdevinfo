Using Quick Data Search


The new Quick Data Search feature allows users in a hurry to type in keywords and to automatically find the list of available indicators or areas containing that keyword via a drop-down menu. While the web-based DevInfo 7 has kept the popular "Browse for data by Topic or Area" feature from previous versions, the Quick Data Search enables fast browsing for information in a database.

Browse for data by topic or area


DevInfo allows you to browse for socio-economic data by going through the list of indicators and geographic regions available in a database.

Using Advanced Search


The Advanced Search feature allows you to search for data by selecting both indicators and areas at the same time.

An overview of visualization tools


Once you have searched for the data you want by specifying the desired indicators or areas, you can use DevInfo to visualize your data in many exciting ways.

Modifying your data selections


Once you're in the visualization page, the left panel will allow you to change your underlying data selections at any time. The options available in the left panel will vary depending on the visualization selected, as well as the underlying data parameters.

Customizing tables in the visualization panel


After making your search selection and clicking "Visualize," you can generate a table and customize it by clicking on the "Settings" tab to access a range of editing options. 

For example, you can choose to only view data for most recent time period, or to view area names in ascending/descending order.

Customizing graphs and charts in the visualization panel


After making your search selections and clicking "Visualize," you can choose from various graphs and charts options to visualize your data. Depending on the underlying data structure based on your selection, certain graphs/charts may be disabled. 

You can remove certain areas from your graphs, modify your time period selections, and select which subgroups (if available) you'd like to view. You can save, download, and share your visualization at any time.

Understanding the different types of maps


Maps are one of the most exciting visualization options in DevInfo, and you can easily customize them to fit your requirements. DevInfo allows you to create four different types of maps: single-thene, multiple-theme, two-dimensional Google Map and three-dimensional Google Map.

Customizing labels, themes and legends for maps

Customize your four map visualizations by learning how to work with the "Settings" tab. This video will show you how to edit labels, themes and legends for maps. For instance, display area name and data values on the maps or modify your map colors.

Creating a multiple-theme map


This video explores how to display multiple indicators on one map. The example taken in this video is a color theme map showing Infant Mortality Rate in the Central African region with a chart overlay showing the Female Literacy Rate.

Downloading and sharing a visualization


DevInfo offers multiple ways for you to download and share your visualizations. For example, if you have a map, you can download it as an image in PNG, JPG, or GIF format, as well as in Excel and KML format. 

Choose the format that best suits your need. This option is useful if you wish to include your visualizations in an external document such as a report or powerpoint visualization.

The Share icon allows you to email your visualizations and share it with your friends/colleagues on Twitter and Facebook. Visualizations are embeddable, and are assigned a specific URL.

Saving visualizations to Gallery


DevInfo allows you to save your visualizations to a private online gallery. All you need to do is create a login account, and enter the name of your visualizations. Keywords and a description will make it easier to find your visualizations later on.

Using the My Data


One of the most talked-about features in DevInfo 7.0 is the new My Data, which allows users to upload data from their own files or to simply copy/paste their data in order to instantly generate powerful visualizations.

Using the catalog to select a database


DevInfo offers fast and easy access to data on human development. The first step is to select the database which you wish to search. In other words, you have to choose where you want to look for your information.
